Wellcome to PAKISTAN The Beautiful Country

Welcome to Pakistan, a land of breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage. From the towering peaks of the Himalayas in the north to the serene beaches of the Arabian Sea in the south, the country's natural beauty is unparalleled. Historical sites like the ancient city of Mohenjo-Daro and the Mughal marvels in Lahore showcase its storied past. The vibrant bazaars, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality of its people make every visit memorable. Pakistan's diverse regions offer a unique and unforgettable experience for every traveler.

Our Locations

Ganga Choti

Ganga Choti, located in the Bagh District of Azad Kashmir, Pakistan, is a stunning mountain peak known for its lush green meadows and panoramic views. Standing at 3,044 meters, it offers a serene escape for nature lovers and hikers. The trek to Ganga Choti is an adventure through picturesque landscapes and dense forests.

gurez-valley

Gurez Valley, nestled in the Himalayas of Azad Kashmir, Pakistan, is a hidden gem known for its pristine beauty and tranquil ambiance. Enveloped by snow-capped peaks and lush meadows, it offers stunning vistas and a serene environment. The valley is a perfect destination for nature enthusiasts and adventure seekers alike.

Attabad Lake

Attabad Lake, located in the Hunza Valley of Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan, is a breathtakingly beautiful lake formed in 2010 due to a massive landslide. Its striking turquoise waters, surrounded by rugged mountains, offer a stunning visual contrast. The lake has become a popular destination for boating, fishing, and sightseeing, attracting visitors from around the world.

Faisal Mosque

Faisal Mosque, located in Islamabad, Pakistan, is an architectural marvel and the largest mosque in the country. Completed in 1986, it features a unique design inspired by a Bedouin tent, with sleek, modern lines and towering minarets. Nestled at the foot of the Margalla Hills, it offers a serene and majestic presence, attracting both worshippers and tourists alike.

Daman-e-Koh

Daman-e-Koh, situated in the Margalla Hills National Park in Islamabad, Pakistan, is a popular viewpoint offering panoramic vistas of the capital city. The spot is a serene escape from the bustling city, surrounded by lush greenery and cool mountain breezes. It's an ideal location for picnics, leisurely walks, and capturing breathtaking photos of Islamabad's skyline

Lake View Park

Lake View Park, located along the Rawal Lake in Islamabad, Pakistan, is a scenic recreational area offering a variety of outdoor activities. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and picnicking amidst beautifully landscaped gardens. The park also features an aviary, a wildlife park, and numerous walking trails, making it a perfect destination for families and nature enthusiasts.

Minar-e-Pakistan

Minar-e-Pakistan, located in Iqbal Park, Lahore, is a towering national monument commemorating the Lahore Resolution of 1940, which paved the way for the creation of Pakistan. This iconic structure stands at 70 meters and combines elements of Islamic, Mughal, and modern architecture. Surrounded by lush gardens and a serene environment, it serves as a symbol of national pride and a popular tourist attraction.

Badshahi Mosque

Badshahi Mosque, located in Lahore, Pakistan, is a majestic Mughal-era mosque renowned for its grandeur and architectural beauty. Completed in 1673 under the rule of Emperor Aurangzeb, it is one of the largest mosques in the world, capable of accommodating over 55,000 worshippers. The mosque features an exquisite blend of red sandstone and marble, with intricate carvings and calligraphy, and stands as a testament to the rich cultural and religious heritage of the region.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border, located between Lahore, Pakistan, and Amritsar, India, is a famous crossing point and the site of the daily Wagah-Attari border ceremony. This ceremony, known for its high-energy display of patriotism and synchronized marching, attracts crowds from both sides of the border. The atmosphere is electrifying as spectators cheer and wave flags, celebrating the spirit of nationalism and camaraderie.
